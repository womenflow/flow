Your alarm jolts you awake. Your phone immediately hands you a to-do list and someone else's bad news. Sound familiar?

There's a gentler way to meet the morning — and it takes about five minutes. These five yoga poses ease your body out of sleep and your mind out of panic, no experience or fancy mat required. (The floor is fine. So is the rug.)

![Gentle morning yoga by the window](/images/flow-s1.png)

## Why Morning Yoga Hits Different
Overnight, your body stiffens and your breathing goes shallow. A few minutes of intentional movement **wakes up circulation, loosens tight spots, and steadies your breath** — which is basically a natural, side-effect-free espresso. Do it before the scrolling starts and you'll feel the difference all day.

## 1. Child's Pose (Start Soft)
Knees wide, big toes touching, fold forward and rest your forehead down. Breathe into your back. This is your "hello, I'm awake now" pose — no pressure, just permission to arrive.

*Hold for 5 slow breaths.*

## 2. Cat-Cow (Wake the Spine)
On hands and knees, arch and round your back with your breath. Inhale, look up; exhale, round down. Your spine has been still for eight hours — this is the stretch it's been begging for.

*Flow for 5 rounds.*

## 3. Downward Dog (The Full-Body Yawn)
Hips up, hands and feet pressing down. Pedal your heels gently. It stretches your hamstrings, shoulders, and back all at once — like a full-body yawn you get to choose.

*Hold for 5 breaths.*

![Woman in an energizing morning stretch](/images/flow-s2.png)

## 4. Low Lunge (Open the Hips)
Step one foot forward, sink the back hip toward the floor. Sitting all day makes hips tight and grumpy — this pose is the fix. Switch sides.

*Hold 5 breaths each side.*

## 5. Standing Forward Fold (Reset)
Stand, hinge at the hips, let your head and arms hang heavy. Sway a little. Feel the tension drain out of your neck and shoulders. Then roll up slowly, one vertebra at a time.

*Hold for 5 breaths.*

## Make It Stick
The whole thing takes five minutes. **The trick isn't doing it perfectly — it's doing it at all, most days.** Roll out of bed, do the five, *then* reach for your phone.

Give it a week. You might find you don't hit snooze quite so hard anymore.
